Information Exfiltration in Federated Learning: Attacks and Defenses
Description
The performance of Machine Learning (ML) largely relies on the availability of large representative datasets. To take advantage of massive data owned by multiple entities, Federated Learning (FL) is proposed. It enables participants to jointly train a global model located on the server without the necessity of sharing their datasets, demonstrating the potential to address the issues of data privacy and data ownership. It has been incorporated by popular machine learning tools such as TensorFlow and PyTorch, and increasingly spreads over various industries. Although the training data are kept within each participant, the server still continues to exchange updates with clients. Hence, there is a potential risk that an adversary could exploit the global model to deduce sensitive information about individual clients or their training data. This results in sensitive information leakage in FL.
